Sophia Foundation for Children

RootSquare partnered with the Sophia Foundation for Children to design and implement an AI-driven system that automates the organization, verification, and matching of child records across images and fragmented documentation.

‍

The Foundation supports children in Kenya and Cyprus, where records are often incomplete, inconsistent, or manually maintained. Photos, names, and background information frequently arrive separately, making it difficult and time-consuming to accurately identify, match, and track children over time. This manual process required extensive human effort and introduced a high risk of duplication, mislabeling, or loss of critical information.

‍

To address this, we built a multimodal, agent-based system that combines computer vision, OCR, language models, and structured reasoning. The system processes both images and accompanying paper documents, extracts relevant information, and intelligently determines whether a child already exists in the database or represents a new record. When uncertainty is detected, the system explicitly flags cases for human review, ensuring accuracy while keeping humans in the loop.

‍

A key innovation of this project was the use of agentic workflows rather than a single monolithic model. Specialized agents handled image embedding and face similarity, text extraction from scanned documents, metadata comparison, and final decision logic. This modular design improved reliability, reduced false matches, and allowed the system to scale as new data was introduced.

‍

The result was a dramatic reduction in manual effort. Tasks that previously required days of sorting, visual inspection, and cross-checking could now be completed in minutes, while maintaining transparency and auditability. Importantly, the system was designed to be robust to inconsistent photo quality and missing documentation, which are common challenges in real-world nonprofit environments.

‍

This collaboration demonstrated RootSquare’s ability to build practical, high-impact AI systems under real operational constraints. It also validated our broader philosophy: AI should not replace human judgment, but support it with precise, explainable tools that reduce workload and improve trust.
